Project Pitching

So, you are thinking about pitching a CSE 611 project! Excellent. This information will help outline the process, align expectations, answer frequently asked questions, and help make the experience better for both you and the project team.

When you submit a project for consideration, student teams will have a chance to review all submitted projects to select which ones are the most interesting for them. It may be that no one is interested at first (don’t lose hope, there is always another semester!). It may be that multiple teams are - in that event, it’s first come, first served for the opportunity to work on the project. All potential Clients will have a chance to “pitch” their project to the class in a lightning style two minute presentation.

A client plays two primary roles - defining the problem, and verifying that the end result solves it. Specify, What problem are you trying to solve? Why is it a problem? Project Planning

Define the project in a way that makes sure you get something delivered that solves your problem, while being achievable by the team in one semester. Follow these steps to optimize that experience of initial requirements definition, and avoid some of the common pitfalls and communication issues that arise during that process.

It’s important that the team understand what problem it is you are trying to solve. As they help design and architect a solution, they will need to make countless small decisions that should be guided by your overall vision, the “Why” behind the project. They may want to jump right into technical talk, but take the time to help them understand what your goals are and why.

Who will use your application? What audience do you expect or want to attract? What and how you build for nontechnical older adults is different than what and how you build for experienced professional workers who will be using a tool every day, which is different than what and how you build for twenty-something computer programmers, which is what the development team will likely consist of. Helping them understand the audience will help them ask questions and make decisions similar to understanding the overall vision.

What process or application is the project supposed to replace? What are your future users doing now to meet the same needs? Maybe you have an old system you’re looking to update to a mobile app. Maybe you have a paper process, or a batch of spreadsheets that you want to automate and streamline. Maybe you are building something entirely new, and hoping that it will drive new behavior that didn’t exist before! In each case, understanding what happens now, and what the problems with that process are, will help the team understand how to improve it with the new project.

This is a difficult step; almost certainly, you would love to have more features and exciting bells and whistles than can be built in one semester. Trying to take on too much usually ends up in a bunch of things that work poorly, rather than a few key things that work well. If you’re an entrepreneur, you already understand the idea of the Minimum Viable Product. What features mean that you can actually use the project to start achieving the goals in the vision? Which ones will make things better, but aren’t mission critical?

Project Developement & Testing

Now that the team is clear on the deliverables, you can plan on meeting regularly to discuss progress, roadblocks and provide feedback based on current developements. This will ensure you are always up-to-date with current progress and the project is headed towards its success.

At a minimum, you should be prepared to meet with the team three or four times during the semester; once or twice to help define the project, once to test the results, and once to take ownership of the final result. You should also be available to periodically answer questions or clarify direction via email during the project. That doesn’t mean that you can’t be more involved, just that you should expect this as the minimum interaction for the project to be able to move forward successfully.

Depending on the project, the background and expertise of the instructor, team, and client, it may be valuable to have checkpoints between the client and instructor to help them guide the team appropriately, or give them some points that they would like emphasized as the project progresses.

Clients that meet more frequently with their teams (in person or via video/phone) have better results, because they are able to provide more frequent and detailed feedback. It can be difficult to envision all the possible paths that a project can take when you are coming up with requirements, and clients that play an active role in guiding the project are almost always happier with the end results.
